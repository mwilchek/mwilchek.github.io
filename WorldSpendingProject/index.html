The purpose for this project is to visualize data using the Google Visualization API and advancing data visualization techniques. This individual project will use country education, healthcare and military spending data as the foundation for the following visualizations. Using creative plots on data, important insights can be drawn from complex data that ultimately tell a story.


Below are the following data sources used in this analysis:

  • Data Title: Healthcare Spending in Millions ($USD)
    Data Source: World Health Organization

Healthcare Spending

Healthcare Summary

Healthcare is a critical need to ensure a country's population growth. Therefore, one would assume the percentage of spending in healthcare would make up a decent fraction of the total GDP of a country. To confirm our assumption let's look at the Healthcare Spending vs Country GDP. Then see how spending has changed over time...





On the table breakdown, we can see the overall spending in healthcare for all the countries. Surprisingly, when compared to the overall GDP of each country, healthcare only makes up a small percentage. Some of the most developed countries are near the bottom like the United States and United Kingdom. For the U.S. this makes sense because most healthcare spending comes from private industry and the most if not all the healthcare spending by government is for Medicare (federal health insurance for people 65 years of age or older) or Medicaid (very low-income people). It is a little odd though that the UK is so low, since most European countries are known for their governments spending more, like France and Germany.


The line graph shows much more interesting trends though of the data. Even though the United States is the lowest in overall healthcare spending percentage compared to its GDP, spending is greatly increasing over time. It’s likely due to the baby boomer generation of people becoming applicable for Medicare. The increasing trend is likely to continue more so with the large unemployment rate during the COVID-19 pandemic forcing people to lose their employer insurance and lowering their income even more. Spending otherwise, remains to be consistent with no significant increases or decreases compared to the other countries which can be viewed more closely as we interact with the buttons below the chart.




Per Person Healthcare Spending

To confirm that we do not percieve a country spending less through the comparison of total Healthcare Spending Vs. Total GDP, let's further breakdown the metric to per person spending.





The table breakdown now changes some of the country standings when we look at the data from the perspective of per person. For example, China was number three on the table above, but now the third to last. The UK also rose slightly higher. However, the US remains to be on the bottom despite it’s increasing trend. Australia seems to care the most about its citizens when it comes to healthcare as it stands as number one for overall spending and per person spending.


As we interact with each country’s spending trend per person from 2012 to 2018 on this line graph, we see the trends to be consistent from 2012-2018. Therefore, we can surmise that for the most part, the countries have not increased their spending for each individual, but the volume of older people receiving government subsidized medical care is increasing, especially for the United States.

Key Healtchare Insights

By analyzing the healthcare spending important insights can be inferred from the visualizations:

  2. Healthcare spending remains to be a small prioritization for the top 12 G-20 countries. Data for 2019 and 2020 may change these trends dramatically since the COVID-19 pandemic, and hopefully there will be more growth seen in per person spending.


Education Spending

Education Summary

Education is another critical need to ensure a country's population and GDP growth. Therefore, one would assume the percentage of spending in education would make up a sizeable fraction of the total GDP of a country. Similar in our healthcare spending analysis, let’s confirm our assumption by looking at the Education Spending vs Country GDP. Then breakdown how spending has changed over time.





On the table breakdown, we can see the overall spending in education for all the countries. Unlike the first breakdown in healthcare, education makes up a much larger overall percentage of the GDP for each country. The United States is at the top with highest gross average of spending but maintains a similar overall percentage of spending versus GDP of around 5% like all the other countries. However, there is an interesting outlier in our breakdown. Saudi Arabia maintains an average of almost 24% of education spending versus it’s GDP. Upon further research into this statistic, it’s been reported that the country is trying to move away it’s dependency of oil and promote higher learning of its people be able to continue to modernize and become a country of technological innovation.



The line graph provides a further breakdown how countries have prioritized education from 2012 to 2016. Besides Saudi Arabia standing out, which can be removed to auto-scale the graph to the other countries, the remaining line trends remain to be consistent for the other countries. Interestingly, China remains to be the next highest, devoting 6% to 7% of its GDP to education. Brazil was staying around the average of other countries, but in 2016 made a sharp drop to 4.2%. This may have been to different budget plans with their new president. India also made a large decrease from close to 4% to 2.4% from 2013 to 2015/16.

However, the total GDP for a country is also dependent on the population of the country like we realized in our healthcare drill down. To confirm that we do not percieve a country spending less through the comparison of total Education Spending Vs. Total GDP, let's further breakdown the metric to per person spending as well.


Compared to our previous breakdown of per person spending using a stacked area chart for the healthcare spending, the map distribution portrays a different illustration that highlights what countries are indeed prioritizing education spending. What’s immediately striking in the map is China. And of course, Saudi Arabia sticks out again for having a significantly higher per person spending rate.




Fastest Growing Countries in Educational Spending

Now that we understand how important education is for all the countries, let's dive deeper to understand the growth of this category. What countries have increased their spending over time by fixed value and total overall percentage?

By breaking down the difference in spending from year to year for the bar chart above, we can clearly see now how countries have changed their education budgets over time. The first immediate piece noticed is that almost all the countries have had decreased percentage growth from 2012 to 2016. Only China and the United States have managed for the most part to have a near-positive growth rate in education spending, but not very significant. Key Education Insights

By analyzing the education spending important insights can be inferred from the visualizations:

  1. Education is a significantly higher prioritization of spending for the G-20 countries compared to healthcare.
  2. Overall education spending has remained consistent from 2012 to 2016, but for most countries is starting to slowdown in growth.
  3. Saudi Arabia has made education a critical investment to grow its country and lessen its dependency on oil.


Military Spending

Military Summary

As analyzed above, healthcare & education spending only make up a fraction of a country's total GDP. Previously in our executive summary we also saw that military spending almost made up 20% to 40% of the total spending of all the categories. Before diving deeper on this category of spending, let's first understand the total Military Spending Vs. Country GDP. Then see how spending has changed over time.





On the table breakdown, we can see the overall spending in military for all the countries. Unlike the previous breakdowns, military spending for the most part makes up a smaller average percentage of GDP compared to education, but still higher than healthcare. The United States is at the top again with highest gross average of spending but maintains a similar overall percentage that hovers between 4% to close to 2%. However, both the United Kingdom and Saudi Arabia have a significantly higher overall percentage of GDP in military spending. Both the U.S. and UK have a close relationship with Saudi Arabia, therefore it’s likely all three countries collaborate closely for military resources.



The line graph provides another further breakdown how countries have prioritized military spending from 2012 to 2019. Besides the United States standing out, which can be removed to auto-scale the graph to the other countries, the remaining line trends remain to be consistent for the other countries. However, China seems to be significantly increasing it’s spending since 2016. This may likely be due to the latest controversy of island disputes in the South China Sea, but if their growth continues their total mean spending may eventually rival the United States.




Per Person Military Spending

The previous visualization gives us a better idea how military spending is and is not a prioritization for a country. However, the total GDP for a country is also dependent on the population of the country as we learned from our previous analyses in the healthcare and education spending. To confirm that we do not percieve a country spending less through the comparison of total Military Spending Vs. Total GDP, let's again further breakdown the metric to per person spending.

Key Military Insights

By analyzing the military spending important insights can be inferred from the visualizations:

  1. Military spending is a significant higher prioritization of spending for most G-20 countries, but not as high compared to education for others.
  2. Saudi Arabia and the United Kingdom have been greatly increasing their spending in military resources.
  3. Asian countries are also seen to be increasing their growth of per person military spending which could be due to increased tension in the South China Sea.


Final Summary

In the beginning, we looked at a high level the total distribution between the top G-20 countries spending in healthcare, education, and military from approximately 2012 to 2019. After drilling down in all the categories to carefully analyze and visualize, a story can be inferred that paints a picture of prioritized investments for some of the most developed countries in the world. Most importantly, we can see how some of the largest developed countries are maintaining their status based on these spending categories. Below are some of the most impactful observations that may likely change how they are viewed to world and ultimately more successful.

  1. United States: With the largest GDP out of all the countries, the US’s growth as remained steady over all the years. The US continues to balance spending priorities in all categories and has continued to demonstrate its status as a world superpower. Compared to most of the other countries, who can be seen fluctuating over certain years and for certain spending categories as world crises occur.
  2. Saudi Arabia: The country continues to devote high prioritization in military spending equally comparable to that of the United States and the United Kingdom. They also have started to significantly make education a prioritization for the country since 2012 and continue to do so according the latest country budget report by KPMG here.
  3. China: One of the largest most populated countries, China has a higher than average prioritization in education spending and is steadily increasing it’s spending in military resources. In latest world developments, the country has been going through notable internal changes with international policy and attempting to demonstrate its presence internationally through it’s handling of the COVID-19 pandemic (healthcare), dominance over technological advancement (education), and growing presence in the South China Sea (military).

It’ll be certainly interesting to watch these top 3 countries evolve as the world continues to develop…
